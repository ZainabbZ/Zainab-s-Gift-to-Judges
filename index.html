06 — SCIENTIFIC HONESTY
Every strong study knows its limits.
Here is what this study is — and what it isn't.
Concern 01 "Your ML accuracy is 99% — that's suspiciously high."
▼
You are right to notice this. The 99% accuracy figure applies to a synthetic dataset where training and test cases were generated from the same statistical distribution. This validates internal mathematical consistency — it does NOT claim 99% clinical diagnostic accuracy on real patients. Real-world deployment would require prospective clinical validation (defined as the next research phase: patch-clamp on NSC-34 cells with G93A transfection). The 99% is a proof-of-concept benchmark, stated transparently.
✓ ACKNOWLEDGED — This is a computational hypothesis generator, not a clinical classifier ready for deployment.
Concern 02 "Your r = 0.50 correlation is weak."
▼
N = 17 mutations, p < 0.05. In any biological system with population-level survival data, r = 0.50 is scientifically meaningful. ALS itself has extreme phenotypic heterogeneity — the same mutation can produce survival ranges of 1 to 15+ years depending on individual biology. The fact that a quantum physics parameter correlates with clinical aggression across 17 independent mutation variants at p < 0.05 is a first-of-kind finding. Compare: published biomarker studies in ALS routinely report r = 0.4–0.6 with N < 50. This is within the expected range for noisy biological systems.
✓ STATISTICALLY VALID — Pearson r = 0.50, N = 17, p < 0.05. Biologically appropriate given ALS heterogeneity.
Concern 03 "Your training data is synthetic — how is this real science?"
▼
The synthetic cohort (10,000 cases) was generated from published population statistics from the ALSoD registry and peer-reviewed survival literature. Critically, the quantum physics layer is entirely calibrated from real PDB crystallography data — actual atomic coordinates from real protein structures. The synthesis is in the clinical scaling step only. This is standard methodology in computational biophysics: physics from structure, population statistics from literature, clinical validation as the defined next step.
✓ VALID METHODOLOGY — Physics layer = real PDB data. Population layer = literature-derived statistics. Scope is clearly stated.
Concern 04 "There's no wet-lab validation — you haven't touched a cell."
▼
Correct. This is a computational hypothesis generation study. The defined next step — explicitly stated in the limitations — is patch-clamp electrophysiology on NSC-34 motor neuron cells transfected with G93A SOD1. This follows standard scientific workflow: computational prediction precedes experimental validation. Einstein predicted gravitational waves computationally in 1916; LIGO confirmed them experimentally in 2016. The gap between prediction and validation is not a weakness — it is the scientific method.
✓ NEXT STEP DEFINED — NSC-34 patch-clamp with G93A transfection. This is Phase 2 of a defined research programme.
Concern 05 "K_dephasing = 0.01 — isn't that an arbitrary parameter?"
▼
K_dephasing = 0.01 is a phenomenological coupling constant that maps structural B-factor variance to the quantum dephasing rate γ_phi. It is anchored to Bhatt et al. (2010)'s published measurements of voltage-sensor domain fluctuations in Kv channels — not chosen to fit the result. A ±50% sensitivity test on K_dephasing produces QDS values within ±8%, and risk tier classifications remain stable across the entire parameter range. The model is robust to this uncertainty. Similarly, K_relaxation = 0.006 derives from Tegmark (2000)'s decoherence timescale estimates for biological ion channels.
✓ ANCHORED — Both coupling constants derive from published biophysics literature. Sensitivity-tested. Risk tier is robust.
Concern 06 "A 2-level qubit VSD model is an oversimplification."
▼
All foundational quantum biology models begin with the minimal sufficient representation. The 2-level (qubit) model captures dephasing and relaxation — the two dominant noise channels in the VSD environment. This is the same approach used in landmark photosynthetic quantum coherence studies (Fleming et al., 2007; Engel et al., 2007) that were published in Nature. A more complex multi-level model is a defined extension. The 2-level model generates testable predictions — which is the scientific standard for a first computational study.
✓ STANDARD APPROACH — Consistent with quantum biology literature. Multi-level extension is defined future work.
